About Manning's Equation

Manning's Equation is an empirical formula used to estimate the average velocity of water flowing in open channels. It was developed by Irish engineer Robert Manning in 1889.

Key Parameters:

  • Q: Discharge (flow rate) in m³/s
  • n: Manning's roughness coefficient (dimensionless)
  • A: Cross-sectional area in m²
  • R: Hydraulic radius = Area / Wetted Perimeter (m)
  • S: Channel slope or energy gradient (m/m)
  • V: Average velocity in m/s

Flow Velocity Standards:

  • V < 0.3 m/s: Very slow flow (may cause sedimentation)
  • 0.3 - 1.0 m/s: Acceptable for most channels
  • 1.0 - 2.0 m/s: Moderate flow (good for many applications)
  • > 2.0 m/s: High velocity (risk of erosion)
